### 🔍 Predicted Errors

#### 1. Proposition 2.3 — Proof Flaw (PRIMARY ERROR)
- **Error Category**: `Equation / proof`
- **Error Location**: Proposition 2.3 (used by Lemma 3, Lemma 4, Theorem 4.1)
- **Confidence**: `0.85`
- **Status**: `ERROR_DETECTED`

**Supporting Evidence**:
The proof of Proposition 2.3 states:

> "On the other hand the identity contraction Min_C(X) → X induces a complete contraction Min_C(X) → Min_R(X). Composing this with R we obtain T_m."

This reasoning is **invalid**. The Min_C(X) and Min_R(X) quantizations are defined using different dual spaces:
- Min_C(X) uses the **complex** dual ball: `‖[x_ij]‖ = sup{‖[φ(x_ij)]‖ : φ ∈ Ball(X*_C)}`
- Min_R(X) uses the **real** dual ball: `‖[x_ij]‖ = sup{‖[ψ(x_ij)]‖ : ψ ∈ Ball(X*_R)}`

Since `Ball(X*_C) ⊂ Ball(X*_R)` for a complex Banach space X, we have `‖·‖_{Min_C} ≤ ‖·‖_{Min_R}`, meaning the identity map `Min_C(X) → Min_R(X)` is **norm-increasing**, NOT a complete contraction. The claim that this is a "complete contraction" conflates the complex and real MIN quantizations.

#### 2. Theorem 4.1 — Invalid Due to Dependency (CASCADING ERROR)
- **Error Category**: `Equation / proof`
- **Error Location**: Theorem 4.1
- **Confidence**: `0.90`
- **Status**: `ERROR_DETECTED`

**Supporting Evidence**:
The proof of Theorem 4.1 states:

> "We endow Y with its (complex) minimal operator space structure... Then Y is a complex operator space, and q is a weak* continuous completely contractive real linear projection **by Proposition 2.3**."

This is the critical dependency. The map `q: Y** → Y` is claimed to be completely contractive on the Min quantizations via Proposition 2.3. Since Proposition 2.3's proof is flawed, the entire proof of Theorem 4.1 — the paper's main result answering Problems 1 and 2 — is **unsupported**.

#### 3. Theorem 3.3 — Minor Typo (NON-CRITICAL)
- **Error Category**: `Equation / proof`
- **Error Location**: Theorem 3.3, part (1)
- **Confidence**: `0.75`
- **Status**: `ERROR_DETECTED` (minor typo)

**Supporting Evidence**:
The proof states `J² = I (since (J²)* = -I)`. Since `J* = L_i` (multiplication by i), we have `(J²)* = L_i² = -I`. By uniqueness of predual maps, `J² = -I_X`, **not** `J² = I`. This is a typographical error (should be `J² = -I`) but does not fundamentally undermine the theorem since `J² = -I` is precisely what's needed for a complex structure.

### 📝 Summary

The paper "A missing theorem on dual spaces" attempts to prove that if a complex Banach space possesses a real predual, then that predual is itself a complex Banach space. However, the proof of **Proposition 2.3** — a technical lemma about minimal operator space quantizations — contains a subtle but fatal flaw. The proposition incorrectly assumes that the identity map between the complex-minimal and real-minimal operator space quantizations is a complete contraction, which is false in general for complex Banach spaces.

Since **Theorem 4.1** (the paper's flagship result) explicitly depends on Proposition 2.3 to establish that the projection `q: Y** → Y` is completely contractive, the main result's proof is **invalid**. This cascades to all subsequent corollaries and theorems that build on Theorem 4.1.

The paper also has a minor typographical error in Theorem 3.3 where `J² = I` should read `J² = -I`, though this does not independently compromise the result.
